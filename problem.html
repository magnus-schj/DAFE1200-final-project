The Challenge

Ensure sustainable use of resources with recycling!

UN's sustainable development goals

In 2016 the United Nations put forth 17 sustainable development goals to prevent drastic changes in the climate by the end of 2030. One of these goals is based on responsible consumption and production. In 2007 the Norwegian government wrote a document where they had a goal for Norway to be the leading country for sustainable development. To reach this goal the population of Norway needs to recycle their waste so it can become new materials in an efficient and eco-friendly way. However, in 2020 we are still not good enough to recycle.

Norwegian waste statistics

In 2019 each citizen of Norway produced 427 kg of waste. The same year only 173,9 kg of waste per person got delivered to recycling. Waste that does not get recycled gets sent to a facility where they burn it all to produce heat and it becomes unusable ash. That is why it is important to recycle so this material does not go to waste, but rather can be recreated into other products. This saves the environment from the extraction of raw material.

Why do people not recycle?

Grønt Punkt Norge did a survey on Norwegian citizens on why they do not recycle. One of the reasons that people often gave was that they don’t get anything back for the time spent on recycling. Check out our solution to this problem.
